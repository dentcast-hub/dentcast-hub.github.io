# DentCast.org ‚Äî Structural SEO & Front-End Audit
**Date:** 2026-05-22  
**Scope:** Static codebase audit of `dentcast-hub.github.io` (custom domain `dentcast.org`). Single-domain.  
**Auditor:** Static-files-only review. No live crawl, no runtime probing.  
**Repo state at audit:** 466 HTML files on disk ¬∑ 432 URLs in sitemap ¬∑ ~447 pages declare `<html lang>`.

---

## Finding #0 ‚Äî Brief-vs-Reality contradiction (must be resolved before other findings can be interpreted)

**Issue:** Brief stated "all user-facing HTML is English; every `<html>` should declare `lang='en'`". Static evidence directly contradicts this.

**Evidence:**
- 446 of 452 HTML pages declare `<html lang="fa" dir="rtl">`.
- Only 6 pages declare `lang="en"` ‚Äî all inside `/metanotes/en/` (5 metanote translations + an English about).
- Every visible `<title>`, `<meta description>`, H1, body copy, OG title/description, and `inLanguage` in JSON-LD on the audited representative pages is Persian.
- `manifest.json` declares `"lang": "fa"`, `"dir": "rtl"`, Persian `description`.
- `llms.txt` describes the platform as "Persian-language scientific dental podcast".

**Implication:** The brief's "English-only, flag every page not `lang='en'`" directive cannot be applied as-is ‚Äî it would label the entire site as defective, which is not the structural defect; the defect is the contradiction itself. This audit proceeds under the empirical reading: **`dentcast.org` is a Persian-language site with a small `/metanotes/en/` English appendix**, and flags real defects under that model. Where applicable, points are also raised about the unfinished bilingual scaffolding.

**Action required from you:** confirm whether the long-term target is (a) Persian primary with English appendix, (b) bilingual with English at root, or (c) English-only. Several findings below tighten or relax depending on the answer.

## 2. Semantic & On-Page Structure

### 2.1 ‚Äî Schema `@id` collision: `Organization` and `Person` share the identical `@id` on `index.html` and `about.html`.
- **Location:** `index.html` lines ~2378-2404 (`@type: Organization` and `@type: Person`, both with `@id: "https://dentcast.org/about.html#person-fouad-shahabian"`); same collision in `about.html` lines ~51 and ~58; every leaf that references this `@id` as `publisher` or `author` propagates the conflict.
- **Severity:** Critical.
- **Why it matters:** Two distinct `@type` nodes sharing one `@id` cause schema parsers to merge them into a single hybrid node with conflicting properties (`founder` of itself, `affiliation` to itself, etc.). Google's structured-data parser silently drops or down-weights such graphs. Because this `@id` is the publisher reference for nearly every page on the site, the damage is site-wide.
- **Evidence:**
  ```json
  { "@type": "Organization", "@id": "https://dentcast.org/about.html#person-fouad-shahabian", ... }
  { "@type": "Person",       "@id": "https://dentcast.org/about.html#person-fouad-shahabian", ... }
  ```
  In `about.html` the Person `affiliation` even points at its own `@id`: `"affiliation": { "@id": "https://dentcast.org/about.html#person-fouad-shahabian" }`.

### 2.2 ‚Äî Schema-type vs. intent mismatch on NoteCast leaves: pages declare `PodcastEpisode` + `PodcastSeries` instead of `Article` with `isBasedOn ‚Üí /episodes/episode-N.html`.
- **Location:** `notecast/episode-10.html`, `notecast/episode-11.html` (and the leaves that have any JSON-LD at all).
- **Severity:** Critical.
- **Why it matters:** A NoteCast page is a *written summary* of an episode, not the episode itself. Duplicating `PodcastEpisode` JSON-LD on the notecast page (without an `@id` distinguishing it from the audio episode's node) creates two pages claiming the same `PodcastEpisode` identity. Google's podcast indexer will pick one and ignore the other; you lose the summary as a discoverable surface. Per the spec you gave, this should be `Article` with `isBasedOn` pointing to the source episode.
- **Evidence:**
  ```
  notecast/episode-10.html schema types: PodcastEpisode, PodcastSeries, Person, Organization
  Expected: Article (+ isBasedOn ‚Üí /episodes/episode-10.html)
  ```

### 2.3 ‚Äî Schema-type vs. intent mismatch on Clinical Insight leaves: pages declare `TechArticle` + `MedicalWebPage` + `WebPage` instead of `MedicalScholarlyArticle` (or at minimum `Article`).
- **Location:** `insight/insight-1.html` (representative).
- **Severity:** Major.
- **Why it matters:** `TechArticle` is for technical-documentation content (API docs, manuals). Clinical reasoning articles based on peer-reviewed literature are exactly the case `MedicalScholarlyArticle` exists for. Using `TechArticle` mis-categorises the section. Stacking `WebPage` + `MedicalWebPage` on the same page also causes type-disambiguation: pick one, not both.

### 2.4 ‚Äî Glossary leaves dual-type as `TechArticle` + `DefinedTerm`; spec was `DefinedTerm` (singular).
- **Location:** All 80 leaves under `glossary/`, e.g. `glossary/bond-strength.html` JSON-LD `@graph`.
- **Severity:** Minor.
- **Why it matters:** The dual typing means each leaf claims to be both a definition and a tech article. Crawlers must pick a primary type. This is structurally consistent across the section (no drift), so the systemic concern is alignment with stated intent rather than glossary-internal inconsistency. Decision needed: drop `TechArticle`, or keep it deliberately as an article wrapper around the term.

### 2.5 ‚Äî Litecast leaves carry `FAQPage` + `MedicalWebPage` but no `Article`/`HowTo`/`CreativeWork` type, leaving the body content untyped.
- **Location:** `litecast/lite-CAST1.html`.
- **Severity:** Major.
- **Why it matters:** Spec said Litecast pages should be `Article` (or more specific). Currently the body of the page is described by no top-level type ‚Äî `MedicalWebPage` covers the page envelope, `FAQPage` covers the Q&A block, but the substantive content (the "2-2-2 rule" article) is structurally invisible to crawlers as an article.

### 2.6 ‚Äî Heading hierarchy violations: H1 ‚Üí H3 skip on Episodes, Insight, Sharehub; H1 ‚Üí H2 ‚Üí H4 skip on NoteCast.
- **Location:** `episodes/episode-1.html` (H1 at 184, H3 at 238 with no H2 between); `insight/insight-1.html` (H1 at 301, H3 at 311); `sharehub/share-1.html` (H1 ‚Üí H3, no H2); `notecast/episode-10.html` (H1 ‚Üí H2 ‚Üí H4, no H3).

## 3. Technical SEO

### 3.1 ‚Äî `manifest.json` declares language/direction (`lang: "fa"`, `dir: "rtl"`) while `index.html` declares `theme-color: #F3F5F7` and `manifest.json` declares `theme_color: "#0b5fff"` ‚Äî installed PWA chrome will not match the website chrome.
- **Location:** `manifest.json` vs. `index.html` `<meta name="theme-color">`.
- **Severity:** Minor (perceptual, not crawl-affecting).
- **Evidence:** Three different `theme-color` values across the site ‚Äî 216 pages `#F3F5F7`, 108 pages `#0b5fff`, 8 pages `#FFD95E`. Browser tab/iOS status-bar colour flickers as users move between sections.

### 3.2 ‚Äî `service-worker.js` has duplicate `fetch` handler code; the second block fires `event.respondWith()` after the first already responded, throwing `InvalidStateError` for every asset request.
- **Location:** `service-worker.js` ‚Äî there are two near-identical "Assets ‚Üí Cache First" blocks inside the same `fetch` listener; the first runs, the second throws.
- **Severity:** Critical.
- **Why it matters:** After SW activation, every non-HTML asset request will log an `InvalidStateError: Failed to execute 'respondWith' on 'FetchEvent': The event has already been responded to`. Browsers tolerate it (the cached or network response from the first call still lands), but: (a) the SW is constantly throwing in production, (b) the second cache-first branch is dead code, (c) the `dentcast-brain.json` exclusion sits between the duplicates and may or may not be reached depending on parse order.
- **Evidence:**
  ```js
  /* üü¢ Assets ‚Üí Cache First */
  event.respondWith( caches.open(CACHE_NAME).then(...) );   // first call

  /* üü¢ Assets ‚Üí Cache First */                              // duplicate
  event.respondWith( caches.open(CACHE_NAME).then(...) );   // second call ‚Äî throws
  ```

### 3.3 ‚Äî `robots.txt` explicitly allows AI training crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, Anthropic-AI, PerplexityBot, CCBot, Google-Extended, Applebot-Extended, etc.), but `llms.txt` declares "License: Educational use with attribution; model training disallowed."
- **Location:** `robots.txt` vs. `llms.txt`.
- **Severity:** Major.
- **Why it matters:** The two files contradict each other. `robots.txt` is the operative signal that AI crawlers actually obey (when they do); `llms.txt` is a human-facing convention with no enforcement. Either you intend to permit training (then drop the licence clause in `llms.txt`) or you don't (then `Disallow: /` for the training-class bots in `robots.txt`). As written, you've published opposite policies in the two files.

### 3.4 ‚Äî `manifest.json` icon paths point to `/favicon-192.png` and `/favicon-512.png`; HTML `<link rel="icon">` uses `/logo-v2.png`. The three "favicon" files (`favicon-32.png`, `favicon-192.png`, `favicon-512.png`) are byte-identical (158,181 bytes each).
- **Location:** `manifest.json` icons; `index.html` head; filesystem `ls -la favicon*.png`.
- **Severity:** Minor.
- **Why it matters:** The three favicons being byte-identical means they're a single image stretched/squashed to fake size variants. Browsers will downscale `favicon-512.png` for tab use anyway, but PWA install will get a blurry/oversized icon. Also: HTML and manifest disagree on which file is the icon, so the cache-warm path is different for browser-shell vs. PWA-install.

### 3.5 ‚Äî Viewport `maximum-scale=1, user-scalable=0` on `index.html` blocks pinch-zoom.
- **Location:** `index.html` line 19.
- **Severity:** Major (accessibility, WCAG 1.4.4).
- **Why it matters:** Disabling user scaling is a documented WCAG failure. Only one page in the codebase carries this declaration (the homepage); 436 other pages use a healthy viewport ‚Äî so this is a one-line fix but currently leaves the most-visited page accessibility-noncompliant.

### 3.6 ‚Äî `episodes/index.html` redirects via `<meta http-equiv="refresh" content="0">`. Static-host limitation acknowledged ‚Äî but the redirect target should at minimum be a section landing, not a `noindex` stub canonicalising to a different URL.
- **Location:** `episodes/index.html`.
- **Severity:** Major.
- **Why it matters:** Meta-refresh is the only redirect tool available on GitHub Pages, but the page's own canonical points away from itself. Combined with ¬ß1.1, this creates a self-conflicting page (refresh-away + canonical-away + breadcrumbs-pointing-at-it).

### 3.7 ‚Äî `noindex` contradiction with structural reachability: `notecast/episode-21.html` has no canonical, no description, and is unreachable from sitemap, yet is not declared `noindex`. It will be crawled as a thin orphan.
- **Location:** `notecast/episode-21.html`.
- **Severity:** Major.
- **Why it matters:** A page with no canonical, no description, no sitemap entry, and no nav scaffolding is exactly the profile of a "thin / low-quality" signal in Google's quality classifier. It still exists at a public URL and will be discovered via internal `/notecast/` links.

### 3.8 ‚Äî `chat.php` committed to a static-only host with placeholder OpenAI credentials.

## 6. Cross-Domain & Infrastructure Issues

### 6.1 ‚Äî Future cross-domain canonical strategy is not declared anywhere; current `hreflang="fa-IR"` declarations to `dentcast.ir` are speculative.
- **Location:** Every leaf carrying `hreflang="fa-IR" href="https://dentcast.ir/..."`.

## Structural Verdict

The site has a credible *intent* (interconnected dental-knowledge ecosystem with structured glossary, episodes, summaries, insights, meta-commentary) but its *implementation* is a flat collection of independently-edited static HTML files without a template layer ‚Äî and the seams are visible everywhere. The most damaging defects are systemic, not cosmetic: a publisher/author `@id` collision propagating through every page's schema graph, a NoteCast floor that is half-orphaned (no nav, no canonicals, no sitemap entries, wrong schema type), a duplicate-handler bug in the service worker, and a cross-section linking lattice that exists only in `llms.txt`. The brief-vs-reality language mismatch (English-stated, Persian-implemented) is the most urgent point to resolve, because it determines whether the existing 446-page Persian corpus is the asset or the legacy to migrate away from.
