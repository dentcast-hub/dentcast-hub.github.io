### 2. Semantic & On-Page Structure

**Issue 2.1-v2** — `Dentist` `@id` is fragmented across files: `index.html` keeps `https://dentcast.org/#clinic` (the legacy id, untouched because it never participated in the collision) while `dentai/dentai-5..16` use the new `https://dentcast.org/#dentist-ahvaz`. Same real-world clinic, two separate `@id`s in the schema graph.
- **Location:** `index.html` line ~2440 (`Dentist` node `@id`) vs `dentai/dentai-5..16` Dentist nodes.
- **Severity:** Major.
- **Why it matters:** A schema graph treats two different `@id`s as two different things. Search engines parsing the site will see two distinct "Ahvaz clinic" entities — the homepage's `#clinic` and the dentai pages' `#dentist-ahvaz`. The local-business signal is split rather than consolidated, weakening map-pack eligibility and review aggregation.
- **Evidence:**
  ```
  index.html       Dentist  →  "@id": "https://dentcast.org/#clinic"
  dentai/dentai-5  Dentist  →  "@id": "https://dentcast.org/#dentist-ahvaz"
  dentai/dentai-10 Dentist  →  "@id": "https://dentcast.org/#dentist-ahvaz"
  ...all dentai-5..16 identical
  ```

**Issue 2.2-v2** — Homepage JSON-LD descriptive fields are written in English on a Persian-primary site.
- **Location:** `index.html` `@graph`: `WebSite.description`, `WebPage (#homepage).name`, `Organization.description`.
- **Severity:** Major (under the new language model).
- **Why it matters:** Under the confirmed Persian-primary model, the descriptive text exposed to search engines and AI crawlers should match the page's actual language (`lang="fa"`, `inLanguage: "fa"`). Right now Google can read the JSON-LD `name`/`description` in English while the page body is Persian — the SERP snippet may surface English text on a Persian result, producing a mismatched listing. The mismatch is also internally self-contradicting: `Organization.description` *says* "Persian-language" while *being written in* English.
- **Evidence:**
  ```
  WebSite.description: "DentCast is a scientific dental education platform and podcast
                        focusing on prosthodontics, dental implants, and evidence-based
                        clinical dentistry."
  WebPage(#homepage).name: "DentCast — Scientific Dental Podcast and Education Platform"
  Organization.description: "DentCast is a Persian-language dental education platform
                              and podcast focused on prosthodontics, implants, and
                              clinical decision-making."
  ```
  Each of these has `inLanguage: "fa"` declared on its containing graph, so the language attribute and the actual text disagree.

**Issue 2.3-v2** — `llms.txt` mixes Persian header with English body.
- **Location:** `/llms.txt` (root).
- **Severity:** Minor.
- **Why it matters:** AI crawlers reading `llms.txt` see a Persian H1 (`# DentCast — پادکست علمی دندانپزشکی`) followed by an English description block (`> DentCast is a Persian-language scientific dental podcast and education platform...`). Same self-contradicting pattern as the Organization.description above. Under the confirmed language model, the body should match — Persian first, with English terminology in-line where useful.
- **Evidence:** First 6 lines of `/llms.txt`:
  ```
  # DentCast — پادکست علمی دندانپزشکی

  > DentCast is a Persian-language scientific dental podcast and education platform by Dr. Foad Shahabian (Prosthodontist)...

  - Author: Dr. Foad Shahabian, Prosthodontist
  - Language: Persian (fa-IR), with English terminology
  ```

## Structural Verdict (v2)

Four targeted commits removed the prior audit's most actively-bleeding defects: the schema `@id` collision that was poisoning every page's publisher/author graph, the service-worker bug that was throwing on every asset request post-activation, the homepage WCAG-failing viewport, the 30 NoteCast/DentCast+ pages that had been gutted of their topbar/audio/canonical/hreflang/schema scaffolding, and the Person self-loop on the homepage. Each fix was scope-disciplined — no incidental edits leaked into adjacent files, and the restoration faithfully reproduced the pre-strip state rather than improvising forward. The site is structurally healthier than the prior audit reflects.

The remaining work is the slower kind: a coherent topical lattice (cross-section linking, populated `DefinedTermSet`, `isBasedOn` on summaries), a deliberate `PodcastEpisode → Article` migration on NoteCast, consolidation of the `#clinic` vs `#dentist-ahvaz` Dentist identity, and a language pass on the JSON-LD/llms.txt descriptive surfaces under the now-confirmed Persian-primary model. None of these are structurally damaging in isolation; collectively they're what separates "an inventory of pages that happen to share a domain" from "an interconnected knowledge ecosystem" — which is the stated product story.
