### 1.3 Top 5 most impactful findings

1. **No `hreflang` mapping between `.ir` and `.org`** anywhere on the site. The 12 pages with `hreflang` use it only to pair Persian and English versions of `metanotes`. Google has zero signal that `.ir` is a regional alternate of `.org`. (Critical)
2. **`sameAs` in Organization/Person schema does not include `dentcast.ir`.** The two domains are not relationally linked in structured data either. (Critical)
3. **`_redirects` contains `/* https://dentcast.org/:splat 301!`** — a wildcard catch-all 301 redirect. If this file is ever read by a Cloudflare Pages / Netlify deployment on the `.ir` path, every page would 301 to `.org`, which is inaccessible inside Iran. Latent footgun. (Critical/conditional)
4. **27 hardcoded `<a href="https://dentcast.org/…">` links across 16 files** (heavy concentration in `notecast/episode-6.html`, `notecast/episode-8.html`, `photocast/index.html`, and `metanotes/en/*`). On `.ir`, these strand users on an unreachable destination. (High)
5. **URL parity mismatch on 10 section hub canonicals vs. sitemap.** Canonicals point to `/<section>/`, sitemap lists `/<section>/index.html`. Plus sitemap includes `episodes/index.html` (which is `noindex`) and `join.html`/`search.html` (no canonical). (High)

### 1.4 Verdict on dual-domain strategy integrity

The **passive layer** of the strategy is in good shape: every indexable page has a canonical to `.org`, every `og:url` and most JSON-LD `url`/`@id` fields use `.org`, internal hrefs are overwhelmingly relative, and `robots.txt` plus the auto-generated sitemap correctly point only at `.org`. Google, encountering an HTML file on `.ir` today, would obey the canonical and consolidate signal to `.org` without major confusion.

The **active layer** is missing. The strategy depends on three positive signals that aren't yet implemented: bidirectional `hreflang` with `x-default → .org` (or equivalent `fa-IR → .ir`, `fa → .org`), `sameAs` in the Organization schema linking the two domains, and a guarantee that no hardcoded absolute `.org` link strands Iranian users. None of the three is in place. The build process has no awareness of the dual-domain split — every tag is hand-authored, and drift from the canonical strategy already exists (notecast/episode-6 alone has nine absolute `.org` links inside body content).

Net: the site won't actively hurt itself in Google's index, but it isn't getting the regional-alternate benefit it could, and the user-experience on `.ir` is broken in several specific places. The strategic gap is roughly 70% policy, 30% execution.

## Section 2 — Pattern Analysis (17 areas)

### 2.1 Canonical tags — ✅ Clean
**Severity:** —  
**Pattern:** 461 of 466 pages carry `<link rel="canonical" href="https://dentcast.org/…">`. All point to `.org`. Zero point to `.ir`. Zero malformed.  
**The 5 pages without canonical:** `404.html`, `player.html`, `search.html`, `join.html`, `google53b100d9b9181a71.html` — all utility/non-indexable. `join.html` carries `noindex,nofollow`, `404.html` has `noindex,follow`. `player.html` and `search.html` are interactive utilities. `google53b…html` is a Google Search Console verification file. All five are reasonable to lack canonicals.  
**Why it matters:** Canonical tags are Google's primary tool for choosing the indexable URL when duplicate content is detected ([Search Central: Consolidate duplicate URLs](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/consolidate-duplicate-urls)). For a dual-domain mirror, a `.org` canonical on a `.ir`-served file tells Google "ignore the `.ir` host, treat the `.org` URL as authoritative."  
**Fix approach:** None needed.

### 2.2 hreflang — ❌ Significant issues
**Severity:** Critical  
**Pattern:** 12 pages carry `hreflang` tags. All 12 use the `fa` ↔ `en` pairing for the metanotes Persian/English mirror. **Zero pages** declare `fa-IR → dentcast.ir` or `x-default → dentcast.org`. The dual-domain strategy is not communicated to Google via `hreflang` at all.  
**Affected page count:** 466 (the entire site lacks dual-domain hreflang); separately, 454 pages lack any `hreflang` at all.  
**Why it matters:** `hreflang` is the only mechanism Google supports for telling it "these two URLs are the same content for different regions, serve each user the right one" ([Search Central: Localized versions](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/specialized/international/localized-versions)). Without it, Google may show `.org` results to Iranian users (who cannot reach them), or may treat `.ir` and `.org` as fully duplicate without the regional-routing benefit. The canonical alone is a weaker, lossier signal than canonical + hreflang.  
**Fix approach:** Inject a 3-line `<link rel="alternate">` block into every template (`<head>`): `hreflang="fa-IR" → dentcast.ir/<path>`, `hreflang="fa" → dentcast.org/<path>`, `hreflang="x-default" → dentcast.org/<path>`. Must be present on **both** domains' serve of each file (bidirectionality is required). Since both domains serve the identical HTML, this works in one pass.

### 2.3 Open Graph & social meta — ⚠️ Partial issues
**Severity:** Medium  
**Pattern:** 454 pages have `og:url` (all `.org`), `og:image` (`.org`), and `twitter:card`. **12 pages do not**: 11 section-level `index.html` files (`chairside/`, `dentai/`, `dentcast-plus/`, `episodes/`, `glossary/`, `insight/`, `litecast/`, `metanotes/`, `notecast/`, `photocast/`, `sharehub/`) plus `google…html`. The `episodes/index.html` is a meta-refresh redirect; the other **10 are real landing pages**. Twitter card is `summary_large_image` on all 454 — consistent.  
**Affected page count:** 10 landing pages with no OG (excluding intentional non-OG files).  
**Why it matters:** Section hubs are among the most likely pages to be shared on Telegram, Twitter, and other social platforms. Without OG, shares render as bare URLs with no preview image. Google does also read `og:title` and `og:description` as fallbacks for SERP snippets when better signals are missing.  
**Fix approach:** Add the standard 10-tag OG block (used elsewhere in the codebase) to the 10 landing hubs.

### 2.4 Schema.org / JSON-LD — ✅ Clean syntactically; ❌ Missing dual-domain linkage
**Severity:** High (for the missing `sameAs` to `.ir`)  
**Pattern:** 460 of 466 pages have at least one JSON-LD block; 767 blocks total; **all 767 are valid JSON** (no parse errors). Schema vocabulary is rich and well-modeled: `PodcastEpisode` (237), `PodcastSeries` (239), `AudioObject` (233), `BreadcrumbList` (454), `FAQPage` (114), `TechArticle` (155), `DefinedTerm` (79), `MedicalWebPage` (66), `MedicalCondition` (46), `Dentist` (14), `Organization` (8), `Person` (8). `mainEntityOfPage` appears in 184 pages.

All structured-data URLs reference `.org`: 3,408 URL fields point to `dentcast.org`, **0 point to `dentcast.ir`**. `sameAs` is declared 18 times with these external profiles:
```
https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/dentcast/id1502032364
https://shows.acast.com/dentcast
https://t.me/dentcast
https://www.instagram.com/dr.foad_shahabian/
```
**Dentcast.ir is not in any `sameAs`.** This is the schema-layer equivalent of the missing `hreflang`.

The 6 pages without JSON-LD are the same utility set (`404.html`, `episodes/index.html`, `google…html`, `join.html`, `player.html`, `search.html`).

**Why it matters:** `sameAs` is Google's entity-graph signal for "these URLs all refer to the same brand/organization/person" ([schema.org/sameAs](https://schema.org/sameAs)). Without `dentcast.ir` in `sameAs`, Google cannot confidently associate the two domains as one publisher.  
**Fix approach:** Add `"sameAs": ["https://dentcast.ir/", ...existing four]` to the `Organization` and `Person` nodes in the global `@graph`. Affects ~8 files (`index.html`, `about.html`, the section hubs) where the Organization schema lives.

### 2.5 Internal links — ⚠️ Partial issues
**Severity:** High  
**Pattern:** Most internal navigation uses relative or root-relative hrefs (e.g., `/glossary/foo.html`, `../episodes.html`) — correct for a dual-host mirror. But a long tail of absolute `<a href="https://dentcast.org/…">` links exists in body content:

- **27 absolute `<a href>` to dentcast.org across 16 files.** Heavy concentration: `notecast/episode-6.html` (9 instances, all glossary cross-links), `notecast/episode-8.html` (2), `photocast/index.html` (1, a "back to home" button), `metanotes/en/*` (10, mostly language-switch and about links), `about.html` (1, the explicit "خارج از ایران" external-mention link).
- **4 absolute `<a href>` to dentcast.ir in 3 files**: `about.html` (the deliberate "داخل ایران" external-mention link), `index.html` (radar callout), and the `mailto:info@dentcast.ir` in `index.html` and `dc-nav.js`.
- **Inline JS hardcoded host:** `global-search.js:125` contains `if (!url.startsWith("http")) url = "https://dentcast.org" + url;` — search results on `.ir` will direct users to `.org` URLs they cannot reach.
- **One malformed URL:** `notecast/episode-6.html:305` contains `https://dentcast.org/glossary/two-step-adhesive-system.` (trailing period, no `.html`).
- **`href="https://dentcast.org/about"` (no `.html`)** in `join.html:241` — relies on `.org`'s server doing extensionless routing.

**Affected page count:** 16 HTML files with absolute `.org` body links; 1 file with malformed URL; 1 JS file with hardcoded host.  
**Why it matters:** On `.ir`, every absolute `.org` link is a dead-end for Iranian users. From Google's perspective, it's also a "link from `.ir` to `.org`" which is fine for consolidating equity, but the UX cost is real. The hardcoded `global-search.js` line means every search-result click from inside Iran fails.  
**Fix approach:** Find-and-replace `https://dentcast.org/` → `/` inside `<a href>` body content. The exception: the deliberate cross-domain mentions in `about.html` (where the URLs are shown as user-facing text) should stay. Verify each. Search script: read the current host at runtime instead of hardcoding.

### 2.6 Sitemap — ⚠️ Partial issues
**Severity:** High  
**Pattern:** Two sitemaps exist:
- `sitemap.xml` — auto-generated by `.github/scripts/gen_sitemap.py`, 463 URLs, all `.org`.
- `sitemap-org.xml` — 235 URLs, all `.org`, appears to be a hand-curated older copy. Both are referenced in `robots.txt`.

`sitemap.xml`'s coverage is good (463 vs. 466 HTML files; the script correctly excludes `404.html`, `player.html`, and Google verification files via its `EXCLUDE_NAMES` / `EXCLUDE_PATTERNS` lists). But there are mismatches against canonicals:

- **10 section hubs have canonical `https://dentcast.org/<section>/` but the sitemap lists `https://dentcast.org/<section>/index.html`.** Different URL string. Google will follow the canonical, so the sitemap entry is redundant — but it sends a slightly noisy signal.
- **13 sitemap URLs have no corresponding page canonical**, including:
  - `episodes/index.html` — which has `<meta name="robots" content="noindex, follow">` and a 0-second `<meta http-equiv="refresh">` to `/episodes.html`. **Should not be in sitemap.**
  - `join.html` — has `noindex,nofollow` but appears in sitemap.
  - `search.html` — utility page, no canonical.
- Two sitemaps in `robots.txt` is harmless but wasteful — `sitemap-org.xml` is a subset of `sitemap.xml` and looks deprecated.

**Why it matters:** Google's sitemap guidance ([Search Central: Build a sitemap](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/sitemaps/build-sitemap)) requires that listed URLs be canonical, indexable, and reachable. A `noindex` page in the sitemap is a conflicting signal and may cause Search Console warnings.  
**Fix approach:** (a) Add `EXCLUDE_PATTERNS` entries in `gen_sitemap.py` for `*/index.html` where a trailing-slash canonical exists, and for known `noindex` pages (`episodes/index.html`, `join.html`, `search.html`). (b) Either delete `sitemap-org.xml` and its `robots.txt` reference, or rename/repurpose it (the name suggests it may have been an early "org-only" sitemap before unification). (c) Decide on URL-form convention: trailing-slash everywhere or `.html` everywhere — current state is mixed.

### 2.8 URL structure parity — ⚠️ Partial issues
**Severity:** Medium  
**Pattern:** Inconsistent URL form across the site:
- 10 section hubs canonical to **trailing-slash** form: `https://dentcast.org/glossary/`, `…/chairside/`, etc.
- 450 content pages canonical to **`.html`** form: `https://dentcast.org/episodes/episode-1.html`.
- Internal hrefs are a mix of `/glossary/foo.html` and `/glossary/foo` (e.g., `notecast/episode-6.html:281` has `href="https://dentcast.org/glossary/dentin-sealing"` — no `.html`).
- Sitemap lists section hubs as `…/index.html` (mismatch with canonical).

GitHub Pages will serve both `/glossary/` and `/glossary/index.html` with 200, creating duplicate content. ArvanCloud's object-storage behavior for directory paths is host-config-dependent and unknown from static files alone.

**Affected page count:** 10 hubs canonically use `/`; 13 sitemap URLs don't match any canonical; an unknown count of internal hrefs use the no-`.html` form.  
**Why it matters:** Inconsistent URL forms for the same resource trigger Google's duplicate-detection ([Search Central: URL canonicalization](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/canonicalization)). Canonicals resolve it, but unnecessary canonicalization weakens consolidated PageRank. Also: paths without `.html` rely on server-side rewriting that may behave differently on `.ir` vs. `.org`.  
**Fix approach:** Pick one form (recommend trailing-slash; cleaner-looking). Standardize canonicals, sitemap, and internal hrefs. This is mostly a sweep of three places.

### 2.9 Title tags — ⚠️ Partial issues

### 2.11 Heading hierarchy — ❌ Significant issues
**Severity:** Medium  
**Pattern:** **One H1 on every page** (excluding 2 intentional misses on `google…html` and `episodes/index.html` redirect). No page has multiple H1s. But **306 pages have heading-level skips** — most of them showing the pattern `[H2, H1, H3, …]` where an `<h2>` for a label appears in the DOM before the page's `<h1>`. Example (`chairside/chairside-1.html`):

```html
<main class="article-content-wrap">
  <h2>Chairside 01</h2>
  <h1>تصمیم‌گیری زیبایی در حضور دیاستم</h1>
```

The template uses `<h2>` as a kicker/eyebrow label above the title. This violates strict logical hierarchy: the first heading in a content region should be the H1.

**Affected page count:** 306 (mostly Chairside, NoteCast, Insight, Glossary patterns).  
**Why it matters:** Google has stated heading-skip is not a direct ranking factor, but it affects accessibility tree construction and assistive-tech navigation ([web.dev: Headings in a logical order](https://web.dev/articles/heading-order)). For accessibility-conscious SEO (and Lighthouse scoring), it's a mild but consistent miss.  
**Fix approach:** Either change the kicker `<h2>` to a styled `<p>` or `<span>` with a CSS class, or swap the DOM order so H1 precedes H2. Template-level fix per content type.

**Fix approach:** Add `loading="lazy"` to all `<img>` tags except the LCP candidate (cover image / hero). Template-level. Add 2-3 `<link rel="preconnect">` for known third-party hosts.

## Section 3 — Detailed Findings (Critical and High)

### 3.1 [CRITICAL] No dual-domain hreflang

**Severity:** Critical  
**Pattern:** The dual-domain mirror strategy is silent in HTML metadata. Google receives no `hreflang` annotation distinguishing the Iranian regional version from the international canonical.

**Example file paths / lines:** All 466 pages — no representative subset, this is universal. The 12 `hreflang`-bearing pages use it for a different purpose:
- `about.html:17-19` — pairs `fa`/`en`/`x-default` for the Persian/English about pages, both on `.org`.
- `metanotes/en/about.html:17-19` — same.
- `metanotes/en/meta-{1..5}.html:8-10` — language pairing.

**Current code (representative — `about.html:17-19`):**
```html
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="fa"       href="https://dentcast.org/about.html">
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en"       href="https://dentcast.org/metanotes/en/about.html">
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://dentcast.org/about.html">
```

**Expected/correct state (principle, not code):** Every page on both domains should declare three hreflang alternates:

```
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="fa-IR"    href="https://dentcast.ir{path}">
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="fa"       href="https://dentcast.org{path}">
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://dentcast.org{path}">
```

### 3.2 [CRITICAL] `_redirects` contains a wildcard 301 to `.org`

### 4.2 Anomalies and observations outside the 17 categories

1. **`Dentcast-brain.txt` and `dentcast-brain.json`** at repo root (267 KB and 297 KB) appear to be content-graph or LLM-context dumps. Not linked from any HTML. Not in sitemap. Probably benign (Google won't crawl them unless linked) but their function is unclear.
2. **`check.json` (224 KB), `dentcast.json` (510 KB), `dentcast-reserve.json` (512 KB), `dentcast-updated.json` (509 KB)** — multiple large JSON snapshots at root. Possible state-file proliferation. Consider moving to `data/` or `.assets/` and gitignoring stale versions.
3. **`llms.txt`** referenced in `<link rel="llms">` on the homepage (`index.html:12`) — `rel="llms"` is not a registered link relation. This is an emerging convention but Google does not parse it. The corresponding `llms.txt` was not located via path probe (worth confirming the file exists and is up-to-date).
4. **`najva-messaging-sw.js`** present — Najva is a Persian push-notification service. Service worker is in the repo. Not directly SEO-relevant but worth knowing for performance budget.
5. **`header-audit.md`** (17 KB) at root — a previous audit document? Suggests this isn't the first SEO pass. If still relevant, integrate its findings.
6. **`Style.css`** capitalized while all other CSS files (`dc-nav.css`, `dc-theme.css`, `global-search.css`) are lowercase. On case-sensitive filesystems (Linux servers, GitHub Actions runners) this can cause silent 404s.
7. **`gitignore..txt`** with the double-dot looks accidental — should it be `.gitignore`?
8. **The `episodes/` directory** contains 206 episode pages but the listing page is at `/episodes.html` (root), not `/episodes/`. The directory's `index.html` is a 0-second redirect. This is fine but unusual — and explains why `episodes/index.html` ended up in the sitemap with a `noindex`. Consider moving the listing into `/episodes/index.html` to flatten the path inconsistency.
9. **`google-search.js` and `global-search.js`** both exist — verify they aren't duplicates or competing for the same hook.

### 4.3 Open questions for the developer

1. Which platforms read `_redirects`? Confirm specifically that the wildcard line cannot fire on either ArvanCloud or Cloudflare's path to `.org`. If neither reads it, consider deleting the file outright.
2. Is `sitemap-org.xml` deprecated? If yes, delete it and the robots.txt reference. If no, what's its purpose distinct from `sitemap.xml`?
3. What's the intended canonical URL form for section hubs — `/glossary/` or `/glossary/index.html`? Lock the convention site-wide, then mechanically align canonical / sitemap / internal hrefs.
4. Are there any pages served only on `.ir` (not in this repo) — e.g., a `/radar` page mentioned in `index.html:2559`'s callout? If so, the `.ir`-only content needs its own SEO strategy and shouldn't be assumed to mirror.
5. For the long-tail content pages (Chairside, NoteCast), is there an editorial owner for title/description rewrites, or should this audit produce a CSV of "current → suggested" for batch review?
6. Should `global-search.js` direct results to whatever host the user is currently on (recommended), or always to `.org` (current behavior, harmful on `.ir`)?
7. The `metanotes/en/*` subtree uses absolute `.org` links throughout — is this intentional (treating the English mirror as a `.org`-only sub-site), or is the intent for it to work on `.ir` too?

### 4.4 What this audit did not verify

- Core Web Vitals (requires real-browser measurement, not HTML parsing).
- Whether `llms.txt`, `Dentcast-brain.txt`, or other large root files are actually crawled, indexed, or serve any function.
